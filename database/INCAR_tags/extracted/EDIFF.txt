<p>Description: <a class="mw-selflink selflink">EDIFF</a> specifies the global break condition for the electronic SC-loop. <a class="mw-selflink selflink">EDIFF</a> is specified in units of eV.
</p>
<p>The relaxation of the electronic degrees of freedom stops if the total (free) energy change and the band-structure-energy change ('change of eigenvalues') between two steps are both smaller than <a class="mw-selflink selflink">EDIFF</a> (in eV). For <a class="mw-selflink selflink">EDIFF</a>=0, strictly <a href="/wiki/index.php/NELM" title="NELM">NELM</a> electronic self-consistency steps will be performed.
</p>
<p>In most cases, the convergence speed is quadratic, so often the cost for the additional iterations is small. Hence, for well converged calculations, we strongly recommend to decrease <a class="mw-selflink selflink">EDIFF</a> to 1E-6. For finite difference calculations (e.g. phonons), even <a class="mw-selflink selflink">EDIFF</a> = 1E-7 might be required in order to obtain precise results. On the other hand, for large systems with many atoms and/or when using <a href="/wiki/index.php/METAGGA" title="METAGGA">METAGGA</a> functionals, attaining an energy convergence of 1E-8 or even 1E-7 might be difficult. So, overall <a class="mw-selflink selflink">EDIFF</a>= 1E-6 is likely the best compromise.
</p>
