<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Format">Format</span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Real_frequencies">Real frequencies</span></h3>
<p>If <b>quartic-scaling GW algorithms</b> are selected, e.g. <a href="/wiki/index.php/ALGO" title="ALGO">ALGO</a>=EVGW0, the first column corresponds to points on the real-frequency axis (in eV). The second and third columns are the real and imaginary parts of the self-energy (in eV) at a given band index and <b>k</b> point. To identify the band index and <b>k</b> point, the ordering has to be taken from the <a href="/wiki/index.php/OUTCAR" title="OUTCAR">OUTCAR</a>: Instead of the QP shifts, a small set of self-energy points are printed to <a href="/wiki/index.php/OUTCAR" title="OUTCAR">OUTCAR</a>, similar to the following output
</p>
<p>Here, the first and second band at the Gamma point are printed. 
The line with <code>selfenergy along real axis</code> contains band No., the KS energy, and the occupation of this state. 
The output in <a class="new" href="/wiki/index.php?title=Vasprun.xml&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Vasprun.xml (page does not exist)">vasprun.xml</a> has the same ordering of bands and <b>k</b> points, i.e., the band index is always the fastest. The frequency grid cannot be controlled. It is always 1000 points in the range of -150 to 150.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Imaginary_frequencies">Imaginary frequencies</span></h3>
<p>For <a href="/wiki/index.php/Practical_guide_to_GW_calculations#Low_scaling_GW_algorithms" title="Practical guide to GW calculations">low-scaling GW algorithms</a>, the QP shifts are evaluated and printed to <a href="/wiki/index.php/OUTCAR" title="OUTCAR">OUTCAR</a> for both, <a class="mw-selflink selflink">LSELFENERGY</a>=T and F. For <a class="mw-selflink selflink">LSELFENERGY</a>=T, the <a class="new" href="/wiki/index.php?title=Vasprun.xml&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Vasprun.xml (page does not exist)">vasprun.xml</a> file additionally contains the self-energy for a given band index and <b>k</b> point on the imaginary-frequency axis:
</p>
<p>To identify the band index and <b>k</b> point, the ordering has to be taken from the QP shifts block in the <a href="/wiki/index.php/OUTCAR" title="OUTCAR">OUTCAR</a>. The band index is faster than the <b>k</b> point.
The imaginary frequency is selected by the Minimax routines<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Kaltak:PRB:2020_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Kaltak:PRB:2020-1">[1]</a></sup>, and the number of points depends on <a href="/wiki/index.php/NOMEGA" title="NOMEGA">NOMEGA</a>.
</p>
