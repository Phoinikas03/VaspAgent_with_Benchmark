<p><a class="mw-selflink selflink">LBERRY</a> = [logical]<br/> Default: <b>LBERRY</b> = .FALSE. 
</p>
<p>Description: This tag is used in the the evaluation of the Berry phase expression for the electronic polarization of an insulating system.
</p>
<p>As of VASP.5.2, calculating the macroscopic polarization and Born effective charges along the lines of the following example (using <a class="mw-selflink selflink">LBERRY</a>=<i>.TRUE.</i> etc) is unnecessary. The use of <a href="/wiki/index.php/LCALCPOL" title="LCALCPOL">LCALCPOL</a> or <a href="/wiki/index.php/LCALCEPS" title="LCALCEPS">LCALCEPS</a> is much more convenient.
</p>
<p>Setting <a class="mw-selflink selflink">LBERRY</a>=<i>.TRUE.</i> in the <a href="/wiki/index.php/INCAR" title="INCAR">INCAR</a> file switches on the evaluation of the Berry phase expression for the electronic polarization of an insulating system, as modified for the application of USPP's and PAW datasets <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-berryformalism1_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-berryformalism1-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-berryformalism2_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-berryformalism2-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-berryformalism3_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-berryformalism3-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-berryformalism4_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-berryformalism4-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-berryformalism5_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-berryformalism5-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-berryultrasoft_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-berryultrasoft-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-berrymmars_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-berrymmars-7">[7]</a></sup>.
