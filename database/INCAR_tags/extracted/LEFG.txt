<p><a class="mw-selflink selflink">LEFG</a> = .TRUE. | .FALSE.<br/> Default: <b>LEFG</b> =  .FALSE. 
</p>
<p>Description: The <a class="mw-selflink selflink">LEFG</a> computes the electric field gradient (EFG) at positions of the atomic nuclei.
</p>
<p>For <a class="mw-selflink selflink">LEFG</a>=.TRUE., the electric field gradient tensors at the positions of the atomic nuclei are calculated using the method of Petrilli <i>et al.</i> <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-petrilli:prb:1998_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-petrilli:prb:1998-1">[1]</a></sup>. 
</p>

</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Recommendations_and_advice">Recommendations and advice</span></h2>
<p>Tight settings are required for calculating the electric field gradient.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Input_parameters">Input parameters</span></h3>
<ul><li>A larger <a href="/wiki/index.php/ENCUT" title="ENCUT">ENCUT</a> value than usual, generally much higher than the value given by ENMAX in the <a href="/wiki/index.php/POTCAR" title="POTCAR">POTCAR</a> file, e.g. 800 eV for C in diamond, rather than the standard 400 eV.</li>
<li>A small <a href="/wiki/index.php/EDIFF" title="EDIFF">EDIFF</a> is required to provide converged chemical shifts, e.g. <code>1E-8</code> eV.</li>
<li>Tighter precision, e.g. <a href="/wiki/index.php/PREC" title="PREC">PREC</a> = Accurate.</li>
<li>Non-spherical contributions to the gradient of the density inside PAW spheres, i.e. <a href="/wiki/index.php/LASPH" title="LASPH">LASPH</a> = .TRUE.</li></ul>
