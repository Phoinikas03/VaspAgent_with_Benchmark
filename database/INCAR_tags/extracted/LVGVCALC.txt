<p><a class="mw-selflink selflink">LVGVCALC</a> = .TRUE. | .FALSE.<br/> Default: <b>LVGVCALC</b> =  .TRUE. 
</p>
<p>Description: <a class="mw-selflink selflink">LVGVCALC</a> switches on calculation of the <i>vGv</i> expression for the orbital magnetic susceptibility.
</p>
<p><a class="mw-selflink selflink">LVGVCALC</a> is available as of VASP.6.4.0.
</p>
<p>When performing a chemical shift calculation the standard <i>pGv</i> susceptibility is calculated and used in the calculation of the CSA tensor <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-yates:prb:2007_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-yates:prb:2007-1">[1]</a></sup>. When <a class="mw-selflink selflink">LVGVCALC</a> is true, the magnetic susceptibility is also calculated with the <i>vGv</i> approximation. <a href="/wiki/index.php/LVGVAPPL" title="LVGVAPPL">LVGVAPPL</a> determines whether the <i>vGv</i> or <i>pGv</i> result is applied in the calculation of the <span class="mwe-math-element"><span class="mwe-math-mathml-inline mwe-math-mathml-a11y" style="display: none;"><math alttext="{\displaystyle \mathbf{G=0}}" xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">

</p>
<p>The <i>vGv</i> expression for the orbital susceptibility was introduced by d'Avezac <i>et al.</i> <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-avezac:prb:2007_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-avezac:prb:2007-2">[2]</a></sup>.
In VASP its ultra-soft generalization is used <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dewijs:havenith:jcp:2021_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-dewijs:havenith:jcp:2021-3">[3]</a></sup>.
