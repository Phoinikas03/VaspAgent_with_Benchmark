<p><a class="mw-selflink selflink">LMAXTAU</a> = [integer] 
</p>
<p>Description: <a class="mw-selflink selflink">LMAXTAU</a> is the maximum <i>l</i>-quantum number included in the PAW one-center expansion of the kinetic energy density.
</p>
<p>The PAW one-center expansion of the density has component up to and including <i>L</i>=2*<i>l</i><sub>max</sub>, where <i>l</i><sub>max</sub> is the <i>l</i>-quantum number of the partial waves on the <a href="/wiki/index.php/POTCAR" title="POTCAR">POTCAR</a> file, with the highest angular moment.
If the PAW one-center expansion of the density has component up to <i>L</i>, then the one-center expansion of the kinetic energy density has components up to <i>L</i>+2.
</p>
<p>This means that as a rule of thumb, for <i>s</i>-elements: <a class="mw-selflink selflink">LMAXTAU</a>=2, for <i>p</i>: <a class="mw-selflink selflink">LMAXTAU</a>=4, and for <i>d</i>: <a class="mw-selflink selflink">LMAXTAU</a>=6.
If you are willing to live with the computational costs, the default for <a class="mw-selflink selflink">LMAXTAU</a> should be safe in all cases, except those involving <i>f</i>-elements.
</p>
