<p><a class="mw-selflink selflink">LCALCEPS</a> = .TRUE. | .FALSE.<br/> Default: <b>LCALCEPS</b> = .FALSE. 
</p>
<p>Description: for <a class="mw-selflink selflink">LCALCEPS</a>=.TRUE. the macroscopic ion-clamped static dielectric tensor, Born effective charge tensors, and the ion-clamped piezoelectric tensor of the system are determined from the response to finite electric fields.
</p>
<p>For <a class="mw-selflink selflink">LCALCEPS</a>=.TRUE., VASP calculates the ion-clamped static dielectric tensor
</p>
<p>the Born effective charge tensors
</p>
<p>and the ion-clamped piezoelectric tensor of the system
</p>
<p>from the <a href="/wiki/index.php/Berry_phases_and_finite_electric_fields#Self-consistent_response_to_finite_electric_fields" title="Berry phases and finite electric fields">self-consistent response to a finite electric field</a> <span style="font-size:16pt"><i>ε</i></span>.
In this case, the "response" of the system is the change in the polarization <b>P</b>, the Hellmann-Feynman forces <b>F</b>, and the stress tensor σ. Mind the <a href="/wiki/index.php/ISIF" title="ISIF">definition/sign convention of the stress tensor</a>.
</p>
<p>If this is combined with <a href="/wiki/index.php/IBRION" title="IBRION">IBRION</a>=6, the contribution from the ionic relaxations to the piezoelectric and dielectric tensors are calculated as well.
