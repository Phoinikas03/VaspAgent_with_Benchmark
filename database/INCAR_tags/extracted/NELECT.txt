<p><a class="mw-selflink selflink">NELECT</a> = [real]<br/> Default: <b>NELECT</b> = number of valence electrons 
</p>
<p>Description: <a class="mw-selflink selflink">NELECT</a> sets the number of electrons.
</p>
<p>The number of electrons is determined automatically from the <a href="/wiki/index.php/POTCAR" title="POTCAR">POTCAR</a> (ZVAL of the element) and <a href="/wiki/index.php/POSCAR" title="POSCAR">POSCAR</a> file (number of the atoms of the respective atom type) assuming the cell is charge-neutral.
If the number of electrons is not compatible with the number derived from the valence and the number of atoms a homogeneous background charge is assumed.
If the number of ions specified in the <a href="/wiki/index.php/POSCAR" title="POSCAR">POSCAR</a> file is 0 and <a class="mw-selflink selflink">NELECT</a>=n, then the energy of a homogeneous electron gas is calculated.
</p>

VASP can automatically correct this leading error, see <a href="/wiki/index.php/Electrostatic_corrections" title="Electrostatic corrections">Electrostatic corrections</a> for further information.
It is important to emphasize that the total energy cannot be corrected for charged slabs, since a charged slab results in an electrostatic potential that grows with the distance from the slab.
This non-convergence of the potential is a result of the interaction between the charged slab and the compensating background.
A practical consequence of this non-convergence is that the total energy depends linearly on the width of the vacuum.
