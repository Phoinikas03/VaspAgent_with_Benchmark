<p><a class="mw-selflink selflink">ML_WTOTEN</a> = [real] 
</p>
<p>Description: Sets a scaling of the fitted potential energy. 
</p>
<p>For <a href="/wiki/index.php/ML_IWEIGHT" title="ML IWEIGHT">ML_IWEIGHT</a>=2 and 3 (default), the potential energy in the training data set is multiplied by <a class="mw-selflink selflink">ML_WTOTEN</a> (unitless). We recommend increasing <a class="mw-selflink selflink">ML_WTOTEN</a> if you plan to apply the force field in a simulation where the accuracy of the total energy is most important. This puts a focus on the energy error and is desirable, for instance, for the computation of defect-formation energies.
</p>
<p>For <a href="/wiki/index.php/ML_IWEIGHT" title="ML IWEIGHT">ML_IWEIGHT</a>=1, <a class="mw-selflink selflink">ML_WTOTEN</a> has the unit of eV/atom, and the potential energy in the training data set is divided by it.
</p>
