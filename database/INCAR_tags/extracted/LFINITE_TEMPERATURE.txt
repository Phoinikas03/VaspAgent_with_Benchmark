<p><a class="mw-selflink selflink">LFINITE_TEMPERATURE</a> = [logical]<br/> Default: <b>LFINITE_TEMPERATURE</b> = .FALSE. 
</p>
<p>Description: <a class="mw-selflink selflink">LFINITE_TEMPERATURE</a> switches on the finite-temperature formalism of many-body perturbation theory for adiabatic-connection-fluctuation-dissipation-theorem (ACFDT)/GW calculations.
</p>
<p>This feature is available as of VASP.6.1.0 for ACFDT/random-phase-approximation (RPA), i.e., <a href="/wiki/index.php/ALGO" title="ALGO">ALGO</a>=ACFDT, ACFDTR, ACFDTRK, and low-scaling <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/index.php/GW_calculations" title="GW calculations">GW calculations</a>, i.e., <a href="/wiki/index.php/ALGO" title="ALGO">ALGO</a>=EVGW0R, GWR[K]. 
</p>
<p>For <a class="mw-selflink selflink">LFINITE_TEMPERATURE</a>=.TRUE., a compressed <a href="/wiki/index.php/Matsubara_formalism" title="Matsubara formalism">Matsubara-frequency</a> grid is used (instead of the zero-temperature formalism of many-body perturbation theory). This allows for GW and RPA calculations for metallic systems. <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Kaltak:PRB:2020_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Kaltak:PRB:2020-1">[1]</a></sup>
