<p><a class="mw-selflink selflink">DEPER</a> = [real]<br/> Default: <b>DEPER</b> = 0.3 
</p>
<p>Description: <a class="mw-selflink selflink">DEPER</a> specifies a relative stopping criterion for the optimization of an eigenvalue.
</p>
<p>The tags <a class="mw-selflink selflink">DEPER</a>, <a href="/wiki/index.php/WEIMIN" title="WEIMIN">WEIMIN</a>, and <a href="/wiki/index.php/EBREAK" title="EBREAK">EBREAK</a> allow fine tuning of the iterative matrix diagonalization, and are best not changed. They are optimized for a large variety of systems, and changing one of the parameters usually decreases performance or can even screw up the iterative matrix diagonalization totally.
In general, these tags control when the optimization of a single band is stopped within the iterative matrix diagonalization schemes:
</p>
<p><a class="mw-selflink selflink">DEPER</a> specifies a relative break-criterion: the optimization of a band is stopped after the energy change becomes smaller than <a class="mw-selflink selflink">DEPER</a> multiplied with the energy change in the first iterative optimization step. The maximum number of optimization steps is always 4.
</p>
