<p><a class="mw-selflink selflink">ODDONLY</a> = .TRUE. | .FALSE.<br/> Default: <b>ODDONLY</b> = .FALSE. 
</p>
<p>Description: <a class="mw-selflink selflink">ODDONLY</a>=.TRUE. selects a subset of <b>k</b>-points for the representation of the Fock exchange potential, with <i>C</i><sub>1</sub>=<i>C</i><sub>2</sub>=<i>C</i><sub>3</sub>=1, and <i>n</i><sub>1</sub>+<i>n</i><sub>2</sub>+<i>n</i><sub>3</sub> odd.
</p>
<p>One may restrict the sum over <b>q</b> in the <a href="/wiki/index.php/Hybrid_functionals:_formalism" title="Hybrid functionals: formalism">Fock exchange potential</a> (or one of its short range counterparts) to a subset, {<b>q</b><sub><b>k</b></sub>}, of the full (<i>N</i><sub>1</sub>×<i>N</i><sub>2</sub>×<i>N</i><sub>3</sub>)  <b>k</b>-point set, {<b>k</b>}, for which the following holds
</p>
<p>where <b>b</b><sub>1,2,3</sub> are the reciprocal lattice vectors of the primitive cell,
and <i>C</i><sub>i</sub> is the integer grid reduction factor along reciprocal lattice direction
<b>b</b><sub>i</sub>.
</p>
<p><a class="mw-selflink selflink">ODDONLY</a>=.TRUE. selects a subset of <b>k</b>-points with <i>C</i><sub>1</sub>=<i>C</i><sub>2</sub>=<i>C</i><sub>3</sub>=1, and <i>n</i><sub>1</sub>+<i>n</i><sub>2</sub>+<i>n</i><sub>3</sub> odd. It reduces the computational work load for HF type calculations by a factor two, but is only sensible for high symmetry cases (such as sc, fcc or bcc cells).
</p>
