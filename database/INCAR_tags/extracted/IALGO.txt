<p>Description: <a class="mw-selflink selflink">IALGO</a> selects the algorithm to optimize the orbitals.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Conjugate_gradient_algorithms">Conjugate gradient algorithms</span></h2>
<p>The band-by-band conjugate gradient algorithms are no longer maintained or supported.
</p>
<ul><li><a class="mw-selflink selflink">IALGO</a>=5-8: Conjugate gradient algorithms</li></ul>
<ul><li><a class="mw-selflink selflink">IALGO</a>=15-18: Conjugate gradient algorithms</li></ul>
<ul><li><a class="mw-selflink selflink">IALGO</a>=28: Conjugate gradient algorithm</li></ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="The_blocked-Davidson_scheme">The blocked-Davidson scheme</span></h2>
<ul><li><a class="mw-selflink selflink">IALGO</a>=38: Blocked-Davidson algorithm (<a href="/wiki/index.php/ALGO" title="ALGO">ALGO</a>=N).</li></ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="RMM-DIIS">RMM-DIIS</span></h2>
<ul><li><a class="mw-selflink selflink">IALGO</a>=44-48: Residual minimization method direct inversion in the iterative subspace (<a href="/wiki/index.php/ALGO" title="ALGO">ALGO</a>= F). <a class="mw-selflink selflink">IALGO</a>=44-48 does not support hybrid functionals.</li></ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Direct_optimization">Direct optimization</span></h2>
<ul><li><a class="mw-selflink selflink">IALGO</a>=53-58: Treat total free energy as variational quantity and minimize the functional completely selfconsistently<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-stich:prb:89_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-stich:prb:89-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gillan:jpc:89_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-gillan:jpc:89-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-arias:prl:92_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-arias:prl:92-5">[5]</a></sup>.</li></ul>
<p>The damped MD algorithm can be combined with <a href="/wiki/index.php/LDIAG" title="LDIAG">LDIAG</a>=.FALSE. to conserve the initial orbital order (when orbitals are read from the <a href="/wiki/index.php/WAVECAR" title="WAVECAR">WAVECAR</a> file).
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Miscellaneous">Miscellaneous</span></h2>
<ul><li><a class="mw-selflink selflink">IALGO</a>=-1: Performance test.</li></ul>
<ul><li><a class="mw-selflink selflink">IALGO</a>=2: Orbitals and one-electron energies are kept fixed.</li></ul>
<ul><li><a class="mw-selflink selflink">IALGO</a>=3: Orbitals are kept fixed.</li></ul>
<ul><li><a class="mw-selflink selflink">IALGO</a>=4: Orbitals are updated by applying a sub-space rotation.</li></ul>
<ul><li><a class="mw-selflink selflink">IALGO</a>=90: Exact Diagonalization. This flag selects an exact diagonalization of the one-electron Hamiltonian. This requires a fairly large amount of memory and should be selected with caution. Specifically, we recommend selecting this algorithm to prepare the <a href="/wiki/index.php/WAVECAR" title="WAVECAR">WAVECAR</a> for RPA or GW calculations, where many unoccupied orbitals are calculated (more than 30-50 % of the states spanned by the full plane-wave basis). To speed up the calculations, we recommend performing a routine ground-state calculation before calculating the unoccupied states.</li></ul>
